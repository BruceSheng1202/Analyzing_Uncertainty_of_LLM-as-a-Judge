You’ll be given a summary of a news article.

Your goal is to evaluate how well this summary captures the heart of the article.

Make sure you fully absorb these instructions before proceeding. Keep this guide open throughout the process so you can refer back to it whenever needed.

Evaluation Criteria:

Relevance (1-5) – Does the summary effectively spotlight the most important information from the article? It should zero in on the essential points without veering into unnecessary or repetitive details. Summaries that include irrelevant content or redundancies should be rated lower.

Steps for Evaluation:

Carefully read both the original article and the summary.
Compare the two, identifying the article's main ideas.
Assess how well the summary conveys these key points and avoids unnecessary information.
Score the relevance of the summary on a scale of 1 to 5.
